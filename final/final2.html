Rome, Italy

Rome is a wonderful place to visit for those looking to travel back in time. There are many archetucural sites, musuems, temples, and fountains to visit. You could spend multiple days here or even just stop by on you way to a smaller village in Italy. No matter what you choose, Rome is a great trip for everyone.

  1. Currently Rome has more than 900 churches!
  2. It is known as the Eternal City.
  3. Rome has a mascot! It's a she-wolf.
  4. Rome has been the capital of Italy since 1870.
  5. There is a PASTA museum!

Paris, France

Known as the City of Love, rome is not short in terms of any acitivity you may want to do. Although most popular with the Fashionistas, you could consider the climb to the top of the Eiffel Tower a hike for those who want a physical challenge.

  1. There are 1,665 steps to the top of the Eiffel Tower.
  2. Paris was once a Roman City!
  3. Paris is also known as the City of Light.
  4. The Eiffel Tower was built for a World Fair.
  5. Some people believe that Paris only has one stop sign... in the whole city.

Tokyo, Japan

Welcome to Tokyo! Where it is believed that there is a vending machine for every 23 citizens. More than vending machines, Tokyo is the capital of Japan is is home to tons and tons of culture. You can find great cuisine, neon lights, and lots of temples. A great trip for an adventurous couple.

  1. Tokyo has more than 37 million residents.
  2. Tokyo is home to the world's largest fish market.
  3. One of the most popular attractions in Tokyo is Disney.
  4. Capsule hotels are very, very common.
  5. They host an annual Cherry Blossom Festival.

New York, New York

Considering there are over 8 million people in New York City, this one may not be for the outdoorsy. A trip to New York has to be on everyone's bucket list. Aside from the Pizza, NYC is also one the fashion capitals of the world and is easily the city with the easiest connections to Europe!

  1. The Statue of Liberty was gifted to the US in 1886.
  2. New York City was the first Capital of the US in 1789.
  3. New Yorkers speak more than 800 languages.
  4. 1 in 38 of USA's population live in NYC.
  5. NYC is also known as the Big Apple.

Los Angeles, California

LA or the city previously called, El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles del Río de Porciúncula, is one of many cities to visit in the US. From, the Hollywood Walk of Fame to Beverly Hills, Rodeo Drive or any other surrounding areas, you will truly never get bored after landing in LAX.

  1. LA has many nicknames, including: City of Angeles, La La Land, and The Big Orange.
  2. The Hollywood sign initally said Hollywoodland!
  3. The Walk of Fame has more than 2,700 stars.
  4. There are lots of tunnels that run along LA streets.
  5. There are many mountain rages in sight from LA and a short drive away.
